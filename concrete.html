Batching Guide

1 bag cement = 50kg = 0.035 m3

1 wheelbarrow = ~0.065 m3 (65 litres)

1 bucket (20L) = 0.02 m3 = 3.5 buckets per bag volume

For C25 1:2:4 per bag: 2 wheelbarrows sand + 4 wheelbarrows ballast + 25L water

Slump Test (Workability)

Equipment: Slump cone (300mm high), tamping rod 16mm, base plate

Target: 50-100mm for normal RC | 25-50mm for pavements | 100-150mm for pumped

Procedure: Fill cone in 3 equal layers, rod each layer 25 times, strike off top, lift cone vertically in 5 seconds, measure slump (drop from original height)

Fail if: Concrete collapses (too wet), shears off (no cohesion), or zero slump (too dry)

Cube Test (Compressive Strength)

Mould: 150mm x 150mm x 150mm steel or cast iron

Sample: Cast minimum 3 cubes per batch

Curing: Submerge in clean water at 20-25C until testing

7-day strength: Should reach 60-70% of design strength

28-day strength: Full design strength (e.g. C25 = 25 N/mm2 minimum)

Testing: Apply load at 0.2-0.4 N/mm2/sec until failure. Calculate: Failure Load / Cube Area

Site Quick Tests

Ball Test: Squeeze handful of concrete - should hold shape, not crumble or bleed water

Trowel Test: Pat surface with trowel - should float smooth, paste should cover aggregate

Water Test: Compact concrete - no free water should rise to surface

Segregation Test: Drop small amount - coarse aggregate should stay mixed in mortar

Temperature: Concrete should be 10-32C at placement (ideal 15-25C)

Curing Requirements

Normal weather: Keep continuously wet for 7 days minimum

Ponding: Create water pond on horizontal surfaces - best method

Hessian/Sacking: Cover with wet hessian, keep constantly damp

Spraying/Fogging: Mist spray 3-4 times daily - don't let surface dry between sprays

Curing Compound: Spray apply immediately after finishing at 1L per 4-5m2

Formwork: Keep in place: columns 2 days, beams/slabs 7-14 days

Hot weather (>30C): Start curing within 30 minutes, use shade/windbreaks

Cold weather (<5C): Use warm water, protect with blankets, keep above 5C for 72hrs

Concrete Admixtures Guide

Waterproofer (Liquid): 200ml per 50kg bag - mix with water before adding

Waterproofer (Powder): 1kg per 50kg bag - mix dry with cement first

Accelerator: 1-2L per 50kg bag - use in cold weather to speed up setting

Retarder: 0.5-1L per 50kg bag - use in hot weather to delay setting

Plasticiser: 150ml per 50kg bag - reduces water by 10-15%, improves workability

Superplasticiser: 0.5-1.5L per 50kg bag - high water reduction, flowing concrete

Bonding Agent (SBR): 1:1 with water as primer, 1:4 cement:SBR slurry for bonding new to old

Curing Compound: Apply 1L per 4-5m2 with sprayer after finishing

Air Entrainer: 50-100ml per 50kg bag - improves freeze-thaw resistance

Shrinkage Reducer: 2-3L per m3 - reduces drying shrinkage cracking

Corrosion Inhibitor: 3-5L per m3 - for marine/saline/coastal environments

Colouring Pigment: 2-5kg per 50kg bag - integral concrete colour

Fibres (Polypropylene): 900g per m3 - reduces plastic shrinkage cracking

Fibres (Steel): 20-40kg per m3 - increases tensile strength for industrial floors
